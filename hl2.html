Half-Life 2

With a game as big as Half-Life a sequel was almost inevitable and Valve definetly delivered on people’s high expectations with ‘Half-Life 2’ being a game that once again blew away its competition with all new cutting edge graphics, real-time physics and unique environments and characters, all wrapped up in a compelling narrative of freeing the earth from alien captors.

The Characters

The Half-Life sequel greatly innovated on its predecessor. The graphics and animations improved greatly over the six short years. A big selling point was that the improved graphical technologies and increased budget allowed for many varied and interesting characters for the player to interact with in the game. The Innovations

Half Life 2’s main technical focus was real-time physics, as a game featuring and revolving around a complex physics simulation hadn’t been achieved yet. The way the game is designed does a lot to emphasise this focus on physics with the player being given a ‘Gravity Gun’ that allows them to pick up objects in the environment and throw them at enemies.

Now almost all games utilise real time physics with objects in game, so this is another experimental innovation by Valve that ended up influencing the videogame industry as a whole.

In order to give updates to their games after they released, Valve developed the digital distribution service ‘Steam’, and Half-Life 2 was the first game they made that required the user to install Steam before it could be played. Steam has now grown into a multi-billion dollar digital distribution service and is the main service people use for purchasing video games for PCs.
